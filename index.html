🧭About Me

I am a Robotics M.S. student at Carnegie Mellon University Robotics Institute, working with the Soft Machines Lab (Prof. Carmel Majidi) and RExLab (Prof. Zachary Manchester). My research explores morphological and physical intelligence — how morphology, materials, and environment can together form self-organized behavior without centralized computation in robotic systems.

I study variable-stiffness aquatic robots and differentiable multiphysics simulation to understand energy transfer and feedback between robots and fluids. My goal is to unify design, control, and physical law in one framework, revealing how embodied intelligence emerges from the coupling of energy, form, and environment.

In the long run, I aim to develop robots that do not just move through environments, but live with them — machines that evolve with their surroundings through material adaptation and distributed control.
